What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight that applies to adult men and women.

Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]Â²

For imperial units: BMI = (weight (lbs) / [height (in)]Â²) Ã— 703

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
  • Obesity Class I: BMI 30 to 34.9
  • Obesity Class II: BMI 35 to 39.9
  • Obesity Class III: BMI 40 or greater

Health Implications

  • Maintaining a healthy BMI reduces risk of chronic diseases
  • BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure
  • Consult healthcare professional for personalized advice
  • Consider body composition, not just BMI
  • Regular exercise and balanced diet are key
